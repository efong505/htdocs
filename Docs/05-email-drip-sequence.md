## Email #2 — The 3 Mistakes
**Send:** Day 2  
**Subject:** The 3 website mistakes costing you customers  
**Goal:** Educate, establish expertise, create urgency

---

Hey {first_name},

After working with dozens of small business owners, I see the same 3 mistakes over and over. They're costing businesses real money — and most owners don't even know they're making them.

**Mistake #1: No clear call-to-action**
Your visitor lands on your homepage. It looks nice. But what are they supposed to DO? If there's no clear next step — call, fill out a form, buy — they leave. And they don't come back.

**Mistake #2: Ignoring mobile**
Over 60% of web traffic in 2026 is mobile. If your site is hard to use on a phone, you're invisible to the majority of your potential customers. Pull out your phone right now and visit your own site. Can you do everything easily with your thumb?

**Mistake #3: Treating your website like a brochure**
A brochure sits there. A revenue-generating website actively works for you — capturing leads, answering questions, building trust, and closing sales 24/7. If your site is just "here's what we do, here's our phone number," it's a brochure.

Here's the thing: all three of these are fixable. And none of them require a complete redesign.

If you haven't already, run through the **Revenue Audit Checklist** in your Survival Kit. It'll show you exactly which of these mistakes apply to your site.

Tomorrow I'll share what a professional website actually costs in 2026 — the real numbers, not the inflated agency quotes.

## Email #5 — DIY vs Hiring a Pro
**Send:** Day 10  
**Subject:** DIY vs hiring a pro — an honest breakdown  
**Goal:** Position professional service without bashing DIY

---

Hey {first_name},

I'm not going to tell you that you NEED to hire a professional for your website. Sometimes DIY is the right call. Here's how to decide.

**DIY makes sense when:**
- You're just starting out and have more time than money
- You enjoy learning tech and don't mind troubleshooting
- Your business model doesn't depend heavily on web leads
- You need a simple "business card" site (hours, location, contact)

**Hiring a pro makes sense when:**
- Your website needs to generate leads or sales
- You've tried DIY and it's not working
- Your time is worth more than the cost of hiring someone
- You need custom functionality (booking, e-commerce, portals)
- Your current site looks dated or unprofessional
- You don't want to deal with updates, security, and maintenance

**The math most people don't do:**

Let's say you spend 40 hours building a DIY site. If your time is worth $50/hour to your business, that's $2,000 in time — plus you end up with a site built by someone who isn't a web professional.

For that same $2,000–$3,000, you could have a professional site that's built to convert visitors into customers from day one.

**The hybrid approach:**

Some of my clients start with a professional build, then I teach them how to maintain it — update content, add blog posts, make small changes. Best of both worlds.

If you're on the fence, the 15-Minute Strategy Session voucher in your Survival Kit is a no-pressure way to get my honest take on your situation.

— Ed
