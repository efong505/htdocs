## Lead Magnet Concept

**Title:** "The Business Owner's Guide to Graphics That Actually Sell"
**Subtitle:** 7 Design Principles That Turn Your Marketing Materials From Forgettable to Profitable
**Format:** PDF, 12-15 pages, designed by Julia (showcasing her own design skills)
**Perceived Value:** $47

### Why This Lead Magnet Works
- Directly relevant to Julia's target audience (business owners who need graphics)
- Positions Julia as the expert — she literally designed the guide
- The guide itself IS a portfolio piece — if it looks amazing, it sells her services
- Actionable content that builds trust before the sales pitch
- Naturally leads into the drip sequence about hiring a graphic designer

### Guide Contents (Outline)

**Cover Page**
- Professional design showcasing Julia's style
- "By Julia Fong — Fine Artist & Graphic Designer"
- juliasgraphicdesign.com

**Introduction (1 page)**
- Why most business graphics fail (they're designed to look pretty, not to sell)
- The difference between art and commercial design
- What you'll learn in this guide

**Principle 1: Start With Your Customer, Not Your Logo (1-2 pages)**
- Your graphics should speak to your customer's problem
- Example: Before/after of a generic flyer vs. a customer-focused one
- Quick exercise: Write down your customer's #1 problem

**Principle 2: Hierarchy — Guide the Eye (1-2 pages)**
- Visual hierarchy determines what people see first
- The F-pattern and Z-pattern for print and web
- Example: Business card with clear hierarchy vs. cluttered one

**Principle 3: Less Is More (1 page)**
- White space is not wasted space
- The "squint test" — if you squint and can't tell what's important, it's too busy
- Example: Clean vs. cluttered marketing materials

**Principle 4: Color Psychology Is Real (1-2 pages)**
- Colors trigger emotions and associations
- Quick reference chart: colors and what they communicate
- How to choose colors that match your brand personality

**Principle 5: Typography Speaks Before Words Do (1 page)**
- Font choices communicate professionalism (or lack of it)
- The 2-font rule
- Fonts to avoid (Comic Sans, Papyrus, etc.)

**Principle 6: Consistency Builds Trust (1 page)**
- Use the same colors, fonts, and style across everything
- The Brand Identity Checklist (bonus — included at the end)
- Example: Consistent vs. inconsistent brand materials

**Principle 7: Professional Graphics Pay for Themselves (1-2 pages)**
- The ROI of professional design
- Case study: How better packaging/design increased sales
- The real cost of "cheap" design (lost customers, lost credibility)

**Bonus: Brand Identity Checklist (1 page)**
- Printable checklist of everything a business needs for consistent branding
- Logo variations, color codes, font names, business card, letterhead, etc.

**About Julia + CTA (1 page)**
- Brief bio
- Services overview
- "Ready to upgrade your graphics? Let's talk."
- Contact info and website
